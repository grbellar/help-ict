01 · Reframe
What this pitch is really about

The tech is easy. Knowing where to go is the hard part.

Wichita already has the shelters, the beds, the case managers, the landlords willing to help. The router that sorts them (the product) is a solved engineering problem. What doesn't exist yet is a community where every single person — from the foster youth aging out tomorrow to the stranger on the sidewalk to the landlord with an empty duplex — already knows the same answer.

03 · Principles
Product-level — govern every asset

Four non-negotiable principles.

Universal

Every Wichitan learns the URL. No segmentation in awareness. A stranger must be able to direct a stranger.

Deterministic

Same input, same output. Every time. I can tell my neighbor to go there and know she'll get the same answer I did.

Simple

Three buttons. Get to the right place. The rest is handled. People in crisis have no bandwidth for nuance — clarity is kindness.

Stigma-free

Help is not charity. We're all in this together. Nobody asks for help if asking is shameful.

04 · Access
One URL, three ways to reach it

The phoneless, the device-less, and the mid-crisis can all get there.

01

Your own phone

QR codes, text-to-browser, or typing six characters. The majority path.

02

Any computer

Wichita Public Library (10 branches), school computers, store kiosks, community centers. Staff briefed to route verbally.

03

Any person

The unlock. When every Wichitan knows the answer, the phoneless can ask anyone, anywhere.

Path 3 is what we're really building. Paths 1 and 2 are already easy. The community is the transmission layer.

Initiative 03
Youth Outreach Kit

No lectures. No wrong questions. Start here.

Foster-youth homelessness jumps 17% → 29% → 42% by age. Broadcast ads fail. Peer voice + physical handoff via trusted partners is the only thing that moves the number.

Hard rule: never use the word "homelessness" in youth-facing copy. "A place to crash." "What happens after 18." The word itself is a bounce risk.

KPI: 1,000 QR scans + 200 tree-starts from youth UTMs by day 90.

07 · Adaptability layer
The disruption comms kit

Marketing that survives a crisis isn't improvised. It's pre-staged.

When Open Door closes for a roof and BRIDGES hits capacity during a severe-weather alert, every partner's communications team is scrambling. Our kit activates automatically — the same way the routing tree flips state instead of rewriting code.

01

Public SMS template

160 chars, pushed by every coalition partner. "Open Door closed. Safe space: Second Light, 1025 N Main. Reply RIDE for pickup."

02

Landlord FAQ letter

What stays the same and what's different for your NEXTenant tenant during the disruption. Mailed + emailed same-day.

03

Case-manager playbook

Laminated one-pager — revised routing, direct lines to call instead of flooded 211, which partners absorbed what.

04

Press holding statement

Pre-approved by all 10 partner agencies. No scramble when a reporter calls at 1:15 PM.

05

DCF 72-hour card

The exact language for a transition-age youth when BRIDGES is full and the 72-hour clock has started.

06

Social holding posts

Pre-designed, drag-and-drop into every partner's Instagram / Facebook / X accounts.

09 · Anti-patterns
Saying no explicitly

What we are not doing.

  • Paid Meta / Google ads to people in crisis. They are not searching — they are walking.
  • Billboards. Indistinguishable from car-accident-lawyer ads.
  • "Faces of homelessness" photo campaigns. Stigmatizing. Landlord-repellent.
  • A giant rebrand. The brand recedes — only the URL shines.
  • Positioning against 211. 211 is overloaded, not broken — we route around it.
  • Any asset that requires helpict.org to be typed from memory. QR, partner cards, printed URLs only.
  • "Awareness" as a KPI. Only recognition + adoption count.
